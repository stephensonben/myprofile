Why I am interested in IT

I enjoy information technology because it is a field that is forever evolving and you constantly need to learn new things.
Like a lot of kids my interest in information technology started with computer games. That changed when I was in my early twenties and studying civil engineering. To support myself I was working part-time for a local government agency and over the years my interest in IT grew as my interest in civil engineering waned. I was fortunate enough to be offered a full time role at that same organisation and have worked there since.

My work within Councils information technology team sees me primarily focused on developing and managing their geographic information systems (GIS) and handling any SQL development or data related tasks.  

I chose to study at RMIT because I wanted to formalise my knowledge and learn as many new things as possible. I am finding in my current role that the expectations of users are growing and I see programming in particular as a way to provide a higher level of service through automation of repetitive tasks, setting up users to self-service their data needs and as a way to reduce overall reliance on information technology staff.

I am hoping to increase my programming ability and learn more about IT in general through my studies.
